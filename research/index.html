About Me

I study how innovators with limited resources leverage online communities and socio-technical systems (e.g. crowdfunding platforms, social media networks, makerspaces) to learn skills and perform their work.

I develop theory and perform human-centered design informed by research in human-computer interaction, crowdsourcing, social computing, and learning sciences. See my research page to learn more about my current projects. I have been supported by the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship, NSF Cyberlearning Grant, and Northwestern’s Segal Design Cluster Fellowship. I previously interned at Microsoft FUSE Labs and Facebook as a HCI/UX Researcher.

I am a PhD candidate in the Delta Lab at Northwestern’s Segal Design Institute where I am advised by Dr. Liz Gerber. Prior to Northwestern, I received my B.S. in Physics with a Minor in Mechanical Engineering from MIT in 2011.

I will be starting a Post-doc at the University of Michigan School of Information in Fall 2017 working with Dr. Tawanna Dillahunt.
